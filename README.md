## What is this?

Accountability Journal is a personal growth app I built for myself because I have a hard time building habits and sticking to my goals.

The first version asked you to reflect on why you missed a habit. That helped — but I realized reflection alone wasn't enough. It kept me looking backward without giving me anything to do differently.

Then I heard someone say: depression is often caused by the gap between who you want to be and who you are now. And the only way to close that gap is with action.

That hit differently. So I rebuilt the whole journaling process around it.

---

## The Core Idea

Instead of asking *"why didn't I do the thing?"*, I wanted the app to ask something better: who do I want to be, who was I actually today, and what will I do next time to get closer?

So I changed it to a system where you put in your goals of who you want to be, check in with what you actually did today, and then the app puts those two side by side so you can see the gap yourself. From there, you're prompted to write an action plan — one concrete thing you'll do next time to get closer to who you want to become.

That's the whole idea. See the gap. Close it with action.

---

## How It Works

### 1. Define your identity

You write identity statements — not to-do lists, not habits, but descriptions of the person you are working toward becoming. For example:

- I am someone who moves their body every day.
- I am a disciplined and consistent reader.
- I am someone who shows up calm and present for the people I love.

### 2. Check in each day

For each identity goal, you write a brief, honest note on what you actually did (or didn't do) today. No checkboxes. No pass or fail. Just the truth.

### 3. See The Mirror

The app puts your identity goal and your daily check-in side by side. You see the gap. The app doesn't judge it — the visibility itself is the mechanism.

### 4. Write an action plan

You're then asked one question:

> *"What is one specific thing you will do next time to get closer to who you want to be?"*

You write a concrete action plan. That's the whole loop.

## Features

- Identity goal creation, editing, deletion
- Daily check-in — freeform log per identity goal
- The Mirror — side-by-side identity vs. actual view
- Action plan prompt and capture
- Photo upload with decorative border stamp
- Weekly and monthly summary reports
- Single-day override (skip without penalty)
- Streak counter per identity goal
- Dark / light mode

---

## What Changed in v2

Version 1 asked: *"What stopped you from doing [habit] today?"*

That question is useful but backward-looking. It can keep you stuck in the problem.

Version 2 replaced it entirely with the identity-gap-action cycle:

| v1 | v2 |
|---|---|
| Habit tracking | Identity goal tracking |
| Missed habit reflection prompt | The Mirror (identity vs. actual, side by side) |
| Why didn't I do the thing? | What will I do next time to close the gap? |
| Backward-looking | Forward-looking |

## Philosophy

This isn't a productivity app. It's a self-concept app. The goal isn't to check more boxes — it's to close the gap between who you are and who you want to become, one honest day at a time.
